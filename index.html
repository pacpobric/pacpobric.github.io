Pac Pobric is an editor at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, where he oversees the Heilbrunn Timeline of Art History.

He is a contributor to the Brooklyn Rail and The Art Newspaper, where he was formerly the Exhibitions editor. A former contributor to the Village Voice, Pobric is also the editor of Matteau! Matteau!, an editorial project about the New York Rangers and the world of hockey.

He has written catalogue essays on Minimalism and its legacy for the Mnuchin gallery and on Sean Scully’s recent work for Cheim & Read.

Pobric has an M.A. in Art History from Hunter College, where he studied with William C. Agee and wrote about Ellsworth Kelly’s years in New York. He lives in the Bronx.
